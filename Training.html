Training

The Department of Palliative Medicine offers postgraduate training, including a DNB Program in Palliative Medicine with two seats per year, starting in 2024. The department also runs a one-year full-time residential Certificate Course in Palliative Medicine, covering principles of palliative care, symptom management, ethics, and communication skills. Training includes outpatient, inpatient, and home care settings, along with peripheral postings in related departments.
In addition to postgraduate education, the department teaches undergraduate students the essentials of palliative care. Postgraduate trainees and fellowship students from specialties like Radiotherapy, Geriatric Medicine, Pulmonary Medicine, and Gynae Oncology also undergo palliative care training. MSc nursing students and Fellowship in Oncology nursing students receive specialized palliative care nursing training, while allied health students in medical sociology and hospital administration spend time in the department.
Doctors and nurses from mission hospitals visit to enhance their skills in providing cost-effective palliative care, particularly in low-resource rural hospitals. The department emphasizes hands-on learning and interdisciplinary collaboration to improve palliative care services across different healthcare settings.
