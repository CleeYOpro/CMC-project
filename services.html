Outpatient Services

Our outpatient clinic is run by a multidisciplinary team consisting of doctors, nurses, social worker, psychologist and chaplain. We provide active symptom management and supportive care to patients at various stages of life at palliative care clinic. Follow up is ensured by systematic appointment at regular intervals. We aim to improve quality of life and relieve suffering. Regular follow-up is ensured.

Inpatient Services

Patients are admitted to palliative care ward for purpose of symptom control, nursing care, terminal care. We also have 6 or 8 palliative caregivers trained by our department who help in patient care. The team consisting of doctor, nurse, chaplain, social worker and psychologist, conducts a comprehensive and detailed bedside round of the morning and evening to each in-patient. Our ward is equipped with all necessary facilities.

Consultation Services

The Department of Palliative Medicine provides consultative services to various wards where patients requiring evaluation and management of distressing symptoms are being cared. We also assist in end of life care and bereavement support. The team consists of doctor, nurse and psychologist.

Palliative Nursing Services

Palliative care nurses focus on treating and implementing strategies to manage symptoms, take care of patients who are confined to bed and handle complex wounds. They also assist in end of life care. The nurses ensure that patients can receive the care they need in the comfort of their own homes, reducing the need for frequent hospital visits during their illness.

Social Care

Palliative care social worker helps in assessment of patients' socio-economic status and provides guidance for financial support through various schemes and procedures as appropriate. This is possible through the generous donations.

Psycho-oncology Services

A palliative care psychologist offers counseling to oncology patients and their families to help them cope with the psychological impact of a life-threatening illness. This service is available to both outpatients and inpatients, ensuring emotional support is accessible to all who need it.

Domiciliary Services

The Department of Palliative Medicine runs a comprehensive home care service for patients who are unable to come to the hospital. The team consisting of specialized home care nurses and conducted after triaging priority for the patients.
Team visit: A team consisting of specialist palliative care physician, nurse, social worker and chaplain visits homes of patients who are unable to come to the hospital. The key goals are to reduce suffering, and provide ongoing care wherever possible (pain & nursing care, spiritual support, social support, bereavement support).
Nurse visit: Those patients who are not ambulatory and bed bound for prolonged periods are requiring nursing interventions are visited by the palliative care nurse.
Social worker's visit: Social worker and chaplain visits the families of patients and assesses the support systems available for the patient.

Hospice

The Department runs a 15-bedded hospice situated 5 km away from the hospital. This is a place of comfort and care for patients who are unable to continue care at hospital or home for various reasons. This is primarily a life-care bed facility for terminally ill patients.

Bereavement Services

The team maintains a continuity of care after death of the patient by providing bereavement support to relatives. In consultation with the family members, those who are at risk of developing prolonged grief disorder are identified, counselled by our psychologist and are guided to take professional help as required.

Spiritual Support Services

Any patient or family with spiritual distress especially nearing end of life are provided with spiritual support and services by the chaplain in the inpatient, outpatient, home and hospice settings.
